Multi-parametric description of drug sensitivity in cancer cell lines

 

Dose-response curves

The shape of the dose-response curve is the most fundamental measure of cellular response to drugs or other perturbing agents. The curve is usually characterized by the IC50, the point at which half-maximal effect is achieved. This is known to vary with drug and cell type, as shown by the red curves, which show the dose-response behavior for of ~50 tumor cell lines to the drug fascaplysin, a natural product and selective inhibitor of the CDK4/cyclin D1 kinase. In a very simple system IC50 correspond to the point of half-maximal drug-target binding.

However, in many cases we observe variation in the maximum effect at the highest drug doses, as illustrated by the dose-response relationships for the microtubule inhibitor and chemotherapeutic drug Docetaxel (blue curves). Variation of this type is not well described by IC50 and remains poorly understood. We have prepared a brief tutorial describing ways of measuring and parameterizing dose-response.

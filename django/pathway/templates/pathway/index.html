As part of our LINCS initiative we are creating a highly curated library of kinase inhibitors, focusing both on clinical compounds that are either NIH approved or are undergoing trials and on highly selective tool compounds against key nodes in the signal transduction pathways.

To facility access to our library of inhibitors, we created a simplified network map of key signal transduction pathways that includes the majority of kinases we can target with small molecules. When searching the complete library, the user will have to search for either individual compounds (link to search function) or for specific kinases (link to search function) to find information on inhibitors and chose them for application. The signaling network map gives a fast overview of the majority of targetable kinases in the context of their signal transduction pathways. This allows users to rapidly find compounds of interest. It also allows users to identify compounds that may have similar effects if they target different proteins in the same signal transduction cascade and it will give immediate clues about the potential immediate biochemical effects of treatment with a given inhibitor.

Clicking on proteins shaded in red give information on all inhibitors with that protein as nominal target. Included is compressed information on the growth inhibition to a small sample of breast cancer cell lines and whether or not the protein is targeted for clinical applications. Clicking through to individual inhibitors links the user back to all information for a given inhibitor contained in our database. {% endblock %}

One component of the HMS LINCS initiative is the creation of a highly curated library of kinase inhibitors. We are focusing on clinical compounds (FDA approved or undergoing trials) and tool compounds which are highly selective for important kinases.

To facilitate access to our library of inhibitors, we created this simplified network map of key signal transduction pathways that includes the majority of kinases we can selectively target with small molecules. This allows biologists to rapidly find compounds which target a given kinase or affect a specific phenotypic outcome, as well as to identify compounds with potentially similar or synergistic effects by virtue of targeting different nodes in the same signal transduction cascade. {% comment %} and it will give immediate clues about the potential immediate biochemical effects of treatment with a given inhibitor. {% endcomment %}

Clicking on proteins shaded in red give information on all inhibitors with that protein as nominal target. Included is compressed information on the growth inhibition to a small sample of breast cancer cell lines and whether or not the protein is targeted for clinical applications. Clicking through to individual inhibitors links the user back to all information for a given inhibitor contained in our database.
